Embedded System Engineer Mar 2019 - Present

@TransferFi

TransferFi is a deep-tech startup focusing on wireless power transfer using RF technologies.

My first project was FPGA implementaion for digital control of phased-array antennas. I used labVIEW to code the I2C protocol communication between the Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) with an I/O Extender. The labVIEW runtime was implemented in an FPGA.

My second project was on firmware development for low-power Industrial Internet of Things (IIoT) applications. Specifically, I used Nordic 52DK and Segger Embedded Studio as the development platform. I have integrated industrial accelerometer and environmental sensors to the nRF52832 and transmit the data through Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) connection to RaspberryPi (RPi). The system have achieved a power consumption of less than 0.3 mWatt.

My third and current project is to implement ModbusTCP on RPi in order to communicate with our client's Programmable Logic Controller (PLC).

Electronic Engineer Jan 2019 - Present

@Project ARMS (Autonomous Robotic Monitoring System)

ARMS is a project under the innovation and design program in NUS aimed to monitor ship equipment such as steering gears, diesel engines, etc.

In the project, I am in charge of the electronic design. This ranges from choosing and sourcing components, firmware development, to PCB design. Specifically, I have developed firmare for the chassis mounted with omniwheels and sensor module responsible for vibration monitoring. Furthermore, I designed the system such that the communication between chassis and sensor module is through bluetooth.

I have also performed market validation and advance the commercial development of the prototype. I have met various industry players and launch our prototype to local start-up competitions to gain industry valudations.

Undergraduate Student Researcher May 2018 - Feb 2019

@Acoustic Research Laboratory

The Acoustic Research Laboratory (ARL) conducts fundamental and applied research on tropical marine acoustics.

I researched on the spatial diversity of acoustic communicaiton underwater up to 100m. Using python as the main simulation platform, I utilized BELLHOP algorithm on 502 phased-array antenna receivers to plot multipath acoustic interference and analyze the correlation between spatial differences and the magnitude spectrum of the BELLHOP result. For more information and simulation code, please visit my github.
